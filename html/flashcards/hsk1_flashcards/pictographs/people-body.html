HSK1 People & Body-Part Pictographs

HSK 1 #1 人 pictograph
Meaning: person
Pinyin: rén (2nd tone)
Frequency: #4 most common
Type: Pictograph (walking figure)
Usage: Core noun for “person/people”
Context: “First human-figure pictograph learned”
一个人 好人
HSK 1 #2 大 pictograph
Meaning: big
Pinyin: dà (4th tone)
Frequency: Top-20 character
Type: Pictograph (arms wide)
Usage: Adjective: size, degree, age
Context: “Visual exaggeration of size”
很大 大小
HSK 1 #3 小 pictograph
Meaning: small
Pinyin: xiǎo (3rd tone)
Frequency: Top-30 character
Type: Pictograph (three tiny dots)
Usage: Adjective for size or endearment
Context: “Contrastive pair with 大”
小狗 小孩
HSK 1 #4 女 pictograph
Meaning: woman/female
Pinyin: nǚ (3rd tone)
Frequency: Core radical
Type: Pictograph (kneeling figure)
Usage: Noun & component in female-related chars
Context: “Foundational radical 女”
女人 女生
HSK 1 #5 子 pictograph
Meaning: child/son
Pinyin: zǐ (3rd tone)
Frequency: Top-50 character
Type: Pictograph (baby with arms)
Usage: Noun & suffix (e.g., 桌子)
Context: “Core family & nominal suffix”
儿子 孩子
HSK 1 #6 目 pictograph
Meaning: eye
Pinyin: mù (4th tone)
Frequency: Common radical
Type: Pictograph (eye shape)
Usage: Radical in vision-related characters
Context: “Visual basis for 看, 睛, etc.”
目光 耳目
HSK 1 #7 手 pictograph
Meaning: hand
Pinyin: shǒu (3rd tone)
Frequency: High-frequency noun/radical
Type: Pictograph (five fingers)
Usage: Hand, actions, measure word
Context: “Key radical 扌 (提手旁)”
手机 洗手
